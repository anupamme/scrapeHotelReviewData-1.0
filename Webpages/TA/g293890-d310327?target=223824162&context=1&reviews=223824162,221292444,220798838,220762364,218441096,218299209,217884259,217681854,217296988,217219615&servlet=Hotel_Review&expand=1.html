“Charming haven”
Reviewed 4 weeks ago

Dwarika's Hotel may just be the best thing in Kathmandu. In the chaos of the city, this place is a real haven. Somehow walking through into the courtyard the sound of the bustle outside disappears and you find yourself in a quiet, historical property. Much of the detail is antique - Mr Dwarika rescued cultural artefacts over the years and built them into the hotel. The rooms are gorgeous, although if you are travelling with friends the "romantic" bathrooms are a little on the open side - be prepared to look away. There is a nice pool and plenty of pool and courtyard tables with cafe and dining service. The food is not cheap - if that's what you're looking for, walk outside - but it's tasty and there's a good deal of variety. Splurge at least one night on the six-course feast. It is a fabulous introduction to Nepali cuisine and an experience in and of itself.
