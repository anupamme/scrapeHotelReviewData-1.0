“Great base for a Big Island adventure”
Reviewed yesterday NEW

We stayed here for five nights as part of a larger Hawaii honeymoon. The location is excellent, at the end of the main road through Kona – a great little town with plenty of shops, bars and good restaurants.

The room was large, comfortable and reasonably well appointed with two comfy beds, large flatscreen TV, and very fast free WiFi. We also had a lovely balcony overlooking the bay. The room was clean and serviced daily. There is plenty of parking available in the secured car park, and quite reasonably priced for the USA.

There is so much to do on the Big Island that we really used the hotel as a base for exploring – for which it is well located. Those who intend to spend their days by the pool may find it on the small side given the size of the hotel, although we never actually had a problem getting a lounger at any time of day. There is also a small private beach with a protected bay which looked great for kids. The poolside bar was good fun while the restaurant was decent. The hotel has its own small general store and coffee shop which is rather convenient.

We had a great stay and if we are ever to travel the 7,200 miles to the Big Island again, then we will consider staying here. Mahalo.

“An Enjoyable Stay”
Reviewed 2 weeks ago

Having come from the excellent Outrigger Napili Shores condo resort in neighbouring Maui (see my review for this), frankly I was a bit reluctant to be staying at a proper hotel again. Thankfully this hotel proved to be a good choice, and a comfortable stay.

Located in Kailua-Kona, it was easy enough to find from the airport, and has plenty located around it, from the shops and decent cafes/restaurants along Ali'i Drive, to your larger stores located just of the highway. Plenty of parking, of which there should be due to the pricey parking charges (my only real grumble about the place).

The room itself was a decent size, very clean, with decent amenities (air-con, large comfy bed, LCD TV, good shower). The 'partial sea view' tag it had was a bit of a joke, in that if you turned your head you could see a bit of the bay brushing up against a concrete wall. But, it was true to an extent. Other rooms were a lot better for this, but probably a lot more expensive. Still, it was quiet, and it suited us.

The hotel itself is a large complex, and has a fair bit on site. There is a small shopping arcade containing an ABC stores mini-mart which was very useful for picking up snacks, drinks etc (at regular prices too, not inflated hotel ones). There is also a little museum/art gallery dedicated to the King himself to whom this hotel is named after, that is worth a look. The gym looked decent, and the pool area and bar is good, although the pool can get run amok with kids sometimes constantly jumping in, despite there being a cosy seating area, so be warned. The little beach area I thought was only so-so, especially as one side of it was cordoned off whist construction work was going on, making it not appear to be very attractive. However the hotel makes the most of what access it has, as sand is clean, and the water is calm enough to swim in, unlike some of the other shoreline access around Kailua. Just don't go expecting a proper beach resort.

As for the restaurants on site, we didn't really try any of them with the exception of the pool bar and grill. The breakfast restaurant appeared deserted for the most part, but with decent places to eat only 5-10 minutes walk away (a few being just on the opposite side of the hotel), and even a good coffee shop within the hotel plaza, you won't feel restricted by what the hotel offers. As mentioned the poolside bar and grill is well worth a visit. They make some very good cocktails (go for the 'Kona Itch'!) and the bar food is of surprisingly good fare. The staff around the hotel are generally friendly and helpful too.

Overall I enjoyed my stay here, and wouldn't have any issues returning. Just wished they wouldn't charge so much for parking.

Stayed August 2014, travelled as a couple
