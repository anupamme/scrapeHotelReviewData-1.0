“Decent accomodation, poor food”
Reviewed yesterday NEW

As everyone else comments, the location of the hotel is excellent and the decent accommodation provided means it is excellent value for money.

The same however can't be said of the breakfast, at least when we stayed. Luke warm, it gave the impression it had been on the warmer for a long time drying out. Perhaps we suffered from going in at a non-busy time so the food wasn't being turned round quick enough, but even then tomatoes served with breakfast are meant to be cooked, not simply sliced in half, and coffee needs to taste like coffee, not just coloured water. I complained about the coffee but was told it was being served in the correct measures.
Also, unless you want a full-English, there is little else on offer.

You are better off going into to one of the nearby restaurants which offer much better breakfasts for the price, albeit without the convenience of being in the hotel of course.

Room Tip: Don't bother eating anything in the restaurant. Much better food at much better prices outside.

“Comfortable”
Reviewed 2 days ago NEW

Very comfortable stay here - we were only here for 1 night but for travellers who are staying longer, I am not sure there would be enough room to hang a weeks worth of clothes on the open wardrobe!
The building is old so there is no air con but there were 2 fans in our room which assisted with keeping the room cool.
We had breakfast at the hotel and it was the usual buffet fare - my only criticism is that the queue for toast was very long and only cooked one side, and pastry selection was disappointing as it only consisted of croissants and a couple of flavours of small muffins - where were the pain au chocolate or Danish pastries?
Overall a good overnight stay. Location great for london eye, Westminster bridge.

Room Tip: We were in room 353 which overlooked an internal stairway - no view!
