If you are looking for a boutique hotel in the old quarters of Istanbul, look no further! Hotel Amira has very nice comfortable rooms, with all the amenities. “5 star service in the heart of the Old City”
Reviewed 4 days ago NEW

We stayed in the Amira Hotel for 2 days in late August, and we loved our stayed. From the moment we arrived, everyone at the hotel treated us like royalty. We sat down for an overview of the city and what to do, they planned tours for us and helped in any way they could.
The boutique hotel is very centrally located, less than 10 minutes walking distance from the Blue Mosque, 20 minutes from the Grand Bazaar. The area was very safe to walk and in general everyone was very helpful.
The hotel is immaculately clean, the rooms are very good sized, modern and clean. The bathroom might be a bit small, but everything worked well, good water pressure, and again everything very clean.
The breakfast and the afternoon tea were delicious, plentiful and with a good variety, nothing to complain about.
Wifi in the hotel was excellent and free, and they gave us a wifi hotspot to take around while around the city, which worked great.
Overall, there's nothing bad I can say about the hotel, I highly recommend it for any visit to Istanbul. Not only is the hotel clean, modern and well located, but the service is top-notch all the way.

“Excellent place to stay”
Reviewed 6 days ago NEW

Hotel Amira has everything that you could wish for in a hotel in this price range - and more! Our room had all the usual facilities (tea and coffee, toiletries, slippers, gowns, hair dryer, safe) and a very comfortable bed (we upgraded to a deluxe room for more space and a jacuzzi bath). All the staff were extremely welcoming, friendly and helpful throughout our stay. Breakfast was delicious with a variety of things to choose from including boiled eggs, scrambled eggs, potatoes, cereal, fresh fruit, pastries, dried fruit etc Afternoon tea was included in the price and was a lovely little "extra" - it was very civilized to be able to return to the hotel after a day of sightseeing and have afternoon tea and then relax on the roof terrace. The hotel is well placed for all the main attractions being a 5-10 minute (uphill) walk to the Blue Mosque, Ayasofya, Topkapi Palace, Basilica Cistern. The tram can be caught from the top of the hill to enable you to go further afield. We stayed here for a week in August and would have no hesitation in recommending the hotel. I would happily stay here again if I were lucky enough to be able to visit Istanbul in the future - it's a wonderful city!

Room Tip: Choose a deluxe room rather than standard for more space.
