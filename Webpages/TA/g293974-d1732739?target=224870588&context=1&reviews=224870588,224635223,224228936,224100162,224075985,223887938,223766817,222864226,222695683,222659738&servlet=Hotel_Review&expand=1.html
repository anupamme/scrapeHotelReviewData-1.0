“Simply superb - read this review!”
Reviewed 3 days ago NEW

Our trip details - 2 nights in August 2014 in double room with balcony. My wife and I went for a few days break in Istanbul. If, like us, you have never been to Istanbul before then this review is for you:

1. You need to be based in the old city. Sultania is simply perfect for this, as it is in some charming narrow streets (traffic-free) five minutes walk from Sultanahmet, which is where you will need to be. It's also very near tram stations which will help you get around cheaply and easily if you want to go further afield.

2. Istanbul is frankly mad and you need an oasis of helpful calm with good English-speaking staff. Sultania has that and is staggeringly quiet. Once in our room we heard nothing. Even the aircon was whisper quiet and easily adjusted.

3. Breakfast was exceptional and varied, served by highly attentive staff. We also had tea and coffee facilities in our room (unusual in Turkey I believe).

4. The room was really big, well equipped and superbly decorated (exactly as shown in the room picture associated with this hotel entry on Trip Advisor). The bathroom was perfect with an enormous walk in shower, great for two people!

5. The roof top terrace restaurant and bar is lovely, has great views of the seafront and Hagia Sofia and, for us as visiting Brits, was really pretty cheap (I guess because the exchange rate works in our favour). Note that alcoholic drinks are European prices but that is normal in Turkey and this hotel is the same as everywhere else in that regard.

6. The restaurants opposite and next to the hotel (one serves good, traditional Turkish (kebabs etc) and the other serves Ottoman cuisine) are both fantastic and really well priced at less than £10 for main courses. There is little need to go further for food for a few days.

Overall this is one of the best hotel experiences I have ever had and I simply cannot fault it with one tiny exception: The 'creamer' provided with the tea and coffee facilities in the room was disgusting and made my tea taste vile. Take your own! In every other respect - PERFECT - and would gladly go back. THank you Sultania.

Room Tip: Use their offered transport to/from the airport. It's reasonably priced and good quality.

“Great all-rounder”
Reviewed 1 week ago

Location: Well positioned between all the main tourist sites. Hagia Sophia, Blue Mosque, Grand Bazaar and Spice Market all easy to reach by foot. Street is relatively quiet – there are some restaurants and cafés on the streets around the hotel but they make little noise.

Rooms: Spacious and decorated in a traditional Turkish style. The rooms on the fourth floor have balconies. The rooms on the fifth floor have roof terraces. We had the choice between both and decided on a balcony room – felt more spacious. There are no views from the rooms. The welcome treat of home-made Turkish Delight in a traditional copper dish was lovely. The rooms are starting to show their age but are still very nice.

Staff: A fantastic team that all speak great English and really do as much as they can to make your stay a good one. The day we checked-in, there was a group of Australians (from Cronulla) who did nothing but complain about things not worth complaining about and the staff took it in their stride and did everything to appease them.
A special mention to Hilmi who helped us with booking the car ferry which can only be done online with a Turkish credit card. He used his own card to book the ferry for us! Fantastic customer service and far beyond expected.

Facilities: There is a small gym, spa and pool in the basement level which was very nice after a long day on our feet.

Dining: Very good breakfast buffet with a mix of Western and Eastern style foods. Eggs cooked to order is always a nice touch. You can eat in the breakfast room or outside in the laneway which we did most days. The rooftop restaurant, Olive, has an incredible view of the Hagia Sophia which seems to be a bit of a secret. They don't advertise the view, just the restaurant, so that benefits hotel guests.

Summary: A very good hotel with good location and everything you need for a short stay in Instanbul.

Room Tip: The fourth floor rooms have balconies
