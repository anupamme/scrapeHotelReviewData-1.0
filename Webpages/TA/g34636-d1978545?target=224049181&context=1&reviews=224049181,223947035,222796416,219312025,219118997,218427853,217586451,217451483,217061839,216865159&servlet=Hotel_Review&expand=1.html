This resort is absolutely gorgeous just needs a restaurant!

“Perfection!”
Reviewed 3 weeks ago

This property is situated on one of the most beautiful beaches in the world and our stay there which was a one week stay could not have been nicer. The condos are just gorgeous and pristine as is the entire property and the beach itself. So many creature comforts and thoughtful amenities. The details in the design of each of these condos are something really special! I came away with a million ideas for my own home! It is truly an exceptional property. The staff was terrific; they were all friendly. They couldn't have been more welcoming or accommodating! I was there with my family and we were there largely to get some serious rest after a very difficult year so we were very content to just relax at the beach every single day. There are kayaks and plenty of things to do there if you feel more ambitious! The pool is small by today's resort standards however it is very, very nice with cabanas, a spa with a waterfall and a little bar situated right next to it. There is a nice fitness room and garage parking for your car. It truly feels like your own private condo on the beach. We stayed in two different units because we extended our stay when we just couldn't bring ourselves to leave. That is just how great it really was. Both units did face the water and the views were amazing from every room. The Hyatt does offer grocery and food delivery and many other services to make the stay amazingly comfortable. We had a rental car there and so we did venture out in the evenings. Siesta Key is very low key and very much a small beach town. You can walk from the Hyatt across Midnight Pass Road to reach a couple of the local restaurants and bars. We had a rental car and so a few different times we ventured out to get our shopping fix and for more upscale dining. A short drive from the Hyatt (20 minutes?) there is terrific resort style shopping at St. Armands Circle where Sarasota meets Longboat Key. There is also a mall en route to Longboat Key however we were not interested in shopping in stores we can shop in at home so we skipped that experience. Euphemia Haye on Longboat Key has to be one of the best restaurants in the area. That drive is a little longer, maybe about 45 minutes from the Hyatt but well worth it. Still, the best part of this vacation was hands down the fact that the Hyatt was perfection. They offered all the comforts of home in a luxury setting on one of the, if not THE best beach in the world! This was our best beach vacation ever!!
