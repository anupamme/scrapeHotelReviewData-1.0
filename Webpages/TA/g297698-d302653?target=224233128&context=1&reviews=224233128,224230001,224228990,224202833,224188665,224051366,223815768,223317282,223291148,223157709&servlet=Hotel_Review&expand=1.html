This was our first trip to Bali and we stayed at the Melia for nearly 2 weeks. We had an amazing time. The facilities are good and the beach side location was superb. We never had a problem finding sun beds and we are not early birds! We stayed in a junior suite which has a mezzanine bedroom and bathroom over the living area and we enjoyed having a little extra space to use. The best part though was the guaranteed view of the gardens. Be sure to request a garden view as some of the rooms at the back of the hotel would not have good views.
For us, though, the two most important elements that made our holiday, were the quality of the food available at such a large hotel and the amazing attitude of all the staff. The training manager of the Melia must be congratulated as the consistency in approach and attitude from all categories of staff were superb. We would recommend learning a few Balinese phrases as people were delighted by our attempts to say thank you, good morning etc.
Our tips for improvement for the hotel would be to upgrade the showers ( at least in our room!) invest in some new music for the beach bar ( there is only so much Adele you can take in one holiday!) and get some more stools for the bar. None of these little points would stop us coming again - we loved it!

“Family trip to Bali”
Reviewed 2 days ago NEW

This was our first trip to Bali as a family. Nusa Dua is a beautiful area which only locals who work in the area are allowed access to. The security checks into Nusa Dua and the hotel were comforting. The Lobby is beautifully presented with the gardens and water features. The room was not what we expected, the brochure from the travel agent gave us the impression that our room would comprise of two rooms which was not the case. We had a loft room with a large staircase, causing significant concerns with our young child. We tried explaining this to the manager who whilst understanding had no family suites available, leaving us with the only option of upgrading to a more expensive room that was not split level. The only benefit to this was we had access to the lagoon pool and the ground floor meaning no lifts up and down all the time. The resort is walking distance to Bali Collections which is where we mainly dined during our stay. The breakfast buffet in the restaurant catered and had a broad range compared to the eating area near the beach which was not as good. The staff were always friendly. Both rooms that we stayed in were beautifully presented and would perfectly suit a couple without kids. The main pool was great, however difficult at times to get a lounge chair as alot of the chairs are reserved very early in the morning. I would still stay at the resort again.

Room Tip: - The hotel offers two bottles of water per room per day without charge. Bali collections next door...

“Good solid family hotel”
Reviewed 5 days ago NEW

I was worried after seeing some of these reviews. We were a family of 4, with two kids under 12. We booked a lagoon access suite on an All Inclusive basis. It wasn't cheap but in total, but it was way cheaper than many other Nusa Dua hotels and well worth it. I last stayed in this hotel back in 1999 and it hasn't changed much, apart from the lagoon access rooms which were added 2 years ago.
The lagoon access rooms, whilst a bit noisy through passing traffic, were absolutely fine. A few minor niggles but nothing major. Could have done with a bit more storage for clothes, but the space suited us all well. We were in building 2 which made the terrace shaded. If you want a sunny terrace, ask for building 1 on the side which would face the sea.
Beds very comfortable. Nice linen. Great soft pillows. TV didn't have the best channels available but there was a DVD player which is a nice touch. Plenty of plugs to charge electronics.
The resort is best suited to families. The pool didn't seem massively big, but surprised us every day as it was never full. Sunloungers - like everywhere - can be a challenge, but we always found some. What made our trip for us was the entertainment available for our children (aged 10 and 11). Every day, they participated in pool aerobics, water polo, water basketball, table tennis, chess. And they never even went in the kids club. For the first time since they were born, I read a book most days and got a tan! They could totally entertain themselves. We ate most evenings at the beachside restaurant with theme nights and live bands/entertainment. To be honest, theme nights and live bands are my version of hell, but my kids loved it. And if my kids are happy, then I am.
If I were travelling solo, or as a couple, I would go somewhere a little quieter and a bit more refined, but the Melia have nailed it. They could upgrade everything, charge the prices of the hotels next door and beyond, but I presume they have chosen not to. It is a lively hotel, a little rough around the edges in places (didn't see the other rooms but lagoon access rooms were light and spacious) but the price fits that. As for our All Inclusive decision....you're given wristbands which show the staff you've paid for AI. Most people hadn't. We spotted two other families the whole time we were there who had. With children who can drink soda and eat ice cream and enjoy international food (rather than the cheaper local food) it was a no brainer for us and fabulous a bill that we were expecting with no extras charged!
Would I go back? Yes - with the kids; but not for a romantic break with my husband!
