“Such a gem! Great location and great value!”
Reviewed 2 weeks ago

Hotel Paris France was an absolute gem. Not only is the building itself charming and beautiful, but it is in a great location, close to several metro stops--making all parts of Paris easily accessible. The closest metro stop is Temple, and it is also near Republique. Every staff member we encountered at the hotel was helpful and friendly. Breakfast is 6 Euros if you want to eat in the hotel. We didn't try the breakfast, as there are many, many cafes nearby where you can grab a croissant and coffee.
The room we stayed in was small but impeccably maintained and very clean. The bed was comfortable, and the crisp, white sheets were changed daily. Despite being on the busy Rue de Turbigo, the double-paned windows keep the noise down pretty well. I would recommend bringing ear plugs and playing the white noise app on your phone at night and in the morning. Because the rooms are packed closely together, it's inevitable that you will hear your neighbors banging around. Also, the wi-fi could be better--our connection kept dropping. The best place to use the wi-fi was in the lobby of the hotel.
In my opinion, this hotel was a fantastic value, and we will stay here again when we come back to Paris.

“Great little hotel and 5* location”
Reviewed 3 weeks ago

Booked this hotel over a year in advance for the finish of Le Tour de France so choices were limited. We've never looked at 2* hotels before but so glad we took a chance on this one. We stayed for 3 nights and had one of our best ever trips to Paris!

The hotel is in an great location - easy to get to by RER B from CDG and just three metro stops from GDN. It's in a lovely neighbourhood. 5-10 mins walk from Pompidou centre with loads of lovely cafs and restaurants nearby on Le Brise Miche. There are a couple of cafe bars near the hotel too which are perfect for a night cap or coffee before bed.

The hotel itself was lovely and clean, all the staff were super friendly and welcoming, and the breakfast croissants and baguettes were very fresh and tasty. Our room was so cosy and quiet - we were on 7th floor which felt private and we had fabulous views, including the top of the Eiffel Tower.

We walked everywhere in Paris but if you prefer to use the metro there are two stations nearby (republique and Temple). There are also bikes (like Boris bikes) on the street, if you're feeling brave! We ate out each night but there are a couple of Monoprix and Carrefour stores nearby (one right behind the hotel).

All in all, a fantastic place to stay in Paris and the only place we'll book in future. Really good value for money.
