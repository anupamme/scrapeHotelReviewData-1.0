“Top notch”
Reviewed 2 weeks ago

Highly recommended. A perfect choice. A short and pleasant walk from all of the iconic sights of old Constantinople. The hotel itself is a charming pair of historic buildings sympathetically linked by a delightful courtyard garden and refurbished to a very high standard. The decor is a modern take on Ottoman opulence. All of the staff were warm and welcoming. Special mention for Ferdi the manager who gave us an excellent briefing when we arrived on where to go and what to do in the (sadly limited) time we had. The trip was a birthday surprise present from my wife, but it was our secret: it was a landmark you'd rather not make too much of a fuss about....When we returned to the hotel on the evening of my birthday however, the staff had bought a cake with candles and sang Happy Birthday! A lovely and heartwarming surprise: they had noticed the date of birth on my passport! Everywhere was spotlessly clean: the towels and bed linen were changed daily and lots of thoughtful touches such as a continuously replenished supply of complimentary bottles of water. The maid didn't just tidy: it was more akin to Feng Shui. The breakfast room was lovely. There was a good selection of food and,uniquely in my experience of hotels world wide, the coffee was really good. Very apparent how well trained the staff were in cleanliness and hygiene. You have to be prepared for stairs: the physical and aesthetic constraints on an historic building like this, mean that a lift is impossible. Be prepared too for an early wake up call from the muezzin calling out from the mosque next door: but this is Istanbul after all. Like Marwa B, I don't do reviews, but this was the only way to express our thanks for making our stay one which will live long in the memory. The highest recommendation I can make is that if we ever go back to Istanbul, we wouldn't consider staying anywhere else.

Room Tip: We had the balcony room with a glimpse view of the Bosphorus and a nice breeze most of the time.
