“Great stay”
Reviewed 2 days ago NEW

This was our first all inclusive experience and overall, it was a great experience.

Food:
The food was really good. I'd say stick to the buffet. We only tried the Italian restaurant and we were not impressed at all. We ended up going to the buffet after because we didn't like/eat the food at the restaurant. Others did say they liked the restaurants so it could just be us. The buffet was more than fine for us, so we were happy to stick with that.

If you do decide to eat at the restaurants, know that they are pretty small and can fill up quickly (especially the first couple of days because people are eager to try them).

We were dissappointed that there wasnt a place we could get food 24hrs. You can order room service at any time but its a bit inconvenient if you're having a good time at the bar.

Beach/Pool
The beach was amazing! The water was so clear and warm. Even as it got later into the afternoon, we would still be in the water.

The hotel staff did a good job at coming around ever so often to see of those at the beach would like any drinks and they brought them in a relatively timely manner

There are a lot of locals who walk the beach trying to sell little nick knacks. If you're not interested, just tell them no and they'll leave you alone

You can also take a nice long walk along the beachfront...its connected to a couple of the other hotels as well and there are little shops and things along the way

The pool bar is the place to be during the day. Jermaine is an awesome guy and was quick to get us whatever we wanted. They host a few games throughout the day that make it entertaining so definitely stick around a bit.

Nightlife/Entertainment:
There's one 24hr bar. Its not very busy late at night but look out for O'neil - he's a great guy and will definitely take care of you.

Guests at the riu palace can eat, drink and party at the riu negril, but they don't have any bars open 24hrs and their club was nothing to get excited about. We went twice and there were hardly any people there. They also closed at 2am and apparently ran out of all alcohol except rum around 1:30 so we ended up just walking back to our hotel.

The entertainment crew is very talented. They put on a show every night and host the day activities. They definitely know how to get people engaged and having fun.
The room:
The room was pretty basic. Comfortable and clean. Nothing extraordinary but we didn't spend much time in the room so we were fine with it. The cleaning staff are amazing. We had Shirley and she must have come into the room 2-3 times a day.


Overall, I would definitely stay there again. We had a great time and felt like our money was very well spent.

“Fun week at the Riu Palace Tropical Bay”
Reviewed 5 days ago NEW

We were there from August 12-19. The Palace is a small resort with 2 small pools. We arrived at the lobby and were given our room keys and 2 towel cards and our bracelets and were on our way. No formal greeting, no welcoming drink. Our porter took us to our suite. We have had hotel rooms larger than this suite. There was no clock, no radio, a small bathroom with a narrow tub with a stand up shower. As I have just recently had a knee replacement and am scheduled for a new hip in September the high ledge entrance to the tub/shower was an adventure. The bed was a full king size bed and not 2 double beds put together as others have noted. The refrigerator was cold and always well stocked. 4 different liquors were there for our use but I only used the rum. None of this was a deal breaker. How much time do you spend in the room anyway?
Dinner started at 6:30 and it was about 4 in the afternoon and we hadn't eaten since early breakfast in Pittsburgh so we looked at the room service menu. The choices were rather limited, so we ventured to the buffet at the opening. We were greeted by Althea. When she found out this was our first time there she gave us a tour of the buffet. Every day she escorted us to out table and remembered what we liked to eat. In conversation we mentioned we were celebrating my wife's birthday and on that day the staff brought out a cake and sang Happy Birthday to her. In the morning Mario was our waiter and always had coffee ready for us at a table near a window. Thanks Mario and Althea you made our dining experience a treat and you, as well as all waiters and waitresses, work hard.
Another problem here is the a a carte reservation system. There are 4 a la cartes but the problem is you have to reserve them for the next day. So, at 8 in the morning, the staff starts taking reservations. We arrived on Tuesday and ate in the buffet. Wednesday morning I arrived near 8 and was the 4th in line and got a reservation for the steak house but the reservation was for the next day. So the first 2 days you eat in the buffet.
We went to the steak house and I had the strip steak and my wife had the lobster. The lobster was delicious she said but the steak was fatty and tough even though I ordered it medium rare. We think the a la cartes should be better than the buffets but after just the one a la carte we decided to stick with the buffets. The buffet always had different choices. There was the American corner with hot dogs, hamburgers and pizza. Saw lots of kids with that. But when in Jamaica eat like a Jamaican. During the week I had curried goat, oxtail, rice and peas, ackee and saltfish, etc. I liked the variety and the small portions offered as I was able to sample so many different things. The desserts deserve a mention on their own. Great mousses, etc. One day there was a chocolate fountain with fruit.
The beach had smooth white sand and the calmest clear water we have ever seen. My wife can't swim but she spent a lot of time in the water with water up to her shoulders. The only problem with the beach is the lack of palapas. We are in our 60's (closer to 70) and now we need shade time when not in the water. There is morning to early afternoon shade with the trees but from mid afternoon on you see people moving their loungers back behind the trees to follow the shade. To me this is a problem. The resort next door has palapas on the beach.
There are vendors passing by often but they were no problem. A couple of times my wife was offered weed. We enjoyed the strolling guitar players and one group of 4 who would stop and play for a couple of dollars. Really a fun beach.
The shows were great. We had several opportunities to talk with the entertainers. A special shout out to Tweetie, Sweetie, Chicken, Popcorn, O. J., the girl D. J. (Sorry, I don't remember your name) and all the others who entertained us all week. These young people are professionals in every sense of the word and everyone should take in a show or 2 the week you are there. We had the same seats every night (right next to Queenie from New York and her group) and really enjoyed the shows. These professionals work hard all day with no air conditioning back stage and on the stage. They practice from 8;30 to 5 every day. They have to know 14 different shows!! Some reviewers complain that they are not broadway shows. 14 different shows each week! Come on people, take in at least one show and watch and applaud. That is the least anyone can do and the entertainers would sure appreciate it.
Chicken led the water aerobics that my wife enjoyed every day. His personality made it fun she said. With my knee replacement and hip replacement to be in September I could not participate. But I watched and was proud of my wife keeping up even though ones half her age were dropping out. Since I am a retired basketball coach she knew what was expected of her.
We at the Palace had exchange privileges with the Riu Negril which was down the beach but separated by another resort. The front desk said the beach was safe to walk at daytime but they did not recommend walking at night. They said taxis were available. Why not a van provided at night so we could go see the shows at both resorts?
All in all it was a pleasant experience because of the beach, the food, and the wonderful people mentioned above as well as everyone else. In all honesty I did not encounter one disgruntled employee. And a note to some of the entertainers who said they read the reviews. Don't give up on your hopes and dreams. You made our week special.

“Love Jamaica but Riu left a lot to be desired”
Reviewed 1 week ago

I'd like to preface this by saying that we have traveled all through the Caribbean and this was our third trip to Jamaica. We've stayed at numerous all-inclusive resorts including Iberostar, Beaches, Occidental Royal Club as well as the Riu Palace in Punta Cana and Riviera Maya and have never had the issues encountered at the Tropical Bay resort. The trip wasn't a total bust so I'll list the pro's first:

The bar and wait staff were amazing. Encountered happy, friendly, accommodating folks in the lobby bar and the buffet restaurant. Althea and Avory welcomed us each evening making sure all nine of us could sit and have dinner together. The food was above average for an all- inclusive resort. There is a nice variety of everything from fresh fruits and salads to gourmet quality desserts. We also had very nice meals in the Steak House as well as Krystal. For the most part, staff all around the resort will great you with a warm, welcoming smile. As I sat in the lobby one evening, Beverly (who was busy keeping the area tidy) approached me with her beautiful smile just to ask if I was enjoying my day. It's the little things like this that would make you want to go back again, HOWEVER, here comes the cons:

We had no clock in our room. Since I didn't bring a watch, computer or cell phone, it was an inconvenience. We had family in three other rooms and all had clocks. Called the front desk to ask for a clock and was told they did not provide clocks in the rooms and if I wanted to know the correct time, I could walk [half way across the resort] to the lobby 24/hrs./day to check their clock!

Housekeeping was also lacking. When our room was made up, we received only bath towels. Each day we had to chase down a housekeeper to get hand towels and we were never able to get wash cloths or floor mats, only excuses.

Our major issue was with the key cards for the doors. Six times during the week (four in one day) we had problems with cards not working. The cards not working were not as annoying as the attitude of the front desk staff. Rather than just addressing the issue, they wanted to know what I had done to the cards. As it turned out, they had cancelled all the cards as if we had checked out of the resort. Had they been apologetic, it wouldn't have been a big deal but when I got attitude on top of ineptitude, enough was enough.

Their reservation system for a la carte restaurants is very inconvenient. You can only make reservations each morning for the following evening. Since we were a party of nine, I got to the lobby by 7am to ensure we would be accommodated. After sitting there for an hour, the staff arrived to begin taking the reservations for the following night. As I was about to make the reservations for my family, a member of the Riu staff ushers a gentleman in front of me. I was told he had an 8am excursion so he needed to make his reservations in a hurry. I was livid. He should have been there, as I was, at 7am or just eat at the buffet.

The evening before we checked out, I made it a point to see the resort managers to address my complaints. It was a huge waste of time. No apologies, lots of excuses. They actually had the nerve to tell me about their great reviews.

If you're going to Jamaica, check out Iberostar. We have stayed there before and should have went back this time. Lesson learned.
