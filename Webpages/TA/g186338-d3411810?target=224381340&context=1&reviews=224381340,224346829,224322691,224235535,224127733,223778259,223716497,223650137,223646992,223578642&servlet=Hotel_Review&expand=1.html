“A great hotel to explore London and get a great night's sleep!”
Reviewed yesterday NEW

We've arrived at Premier Inn late at night and were pleased to find a helpful staff member, willing and eager to get us through check in and get us to our room as quickly as possible.
As we were traveling with two young children and stayed at the hotel for seven nights, we were able to get a good feeling of the place.

Location – the hotel is situated practically in the middle of London's tourists' area. Almost every attraction and place worth visiting is in walking distance (the furthest being about one hour walk from the hotel). Naturally there is a bus stop and a tube station within two minutes of the hotel.

Rooms – our room was extremely spacious, with enough space for our double bed and two twin beds for the kids, and there was still room to spare. Bath was very large and clean. Hot water was available at all hours of the day. The best thing about our room though, was the fact that once we've shut the door behind us, we felt like we were the only guests staying at the hotel. There was absolutely no noise, no disturbance, no voices being herd over thin walls – complete and utter silence. This proved to be a pleasure as we were able to get a decent rest after a day of walking around. The bed and pillows were also very comfortable.

Staff – every single staff member was friendly, eager to help, and welcoming.

Food – we didn’t take breakfast or dinner at the hotel so I really couldn’t say, although there was a long queue for dinner every night so I guess the food was decent.

A couple of minuses – first, we got a room at the basement floor which meant no view and no air circulation (besides the AC). Surprisingly, this wasn’t too much of a burden. Second, there was no safe in the room, which I think is quite elementary.

To sum it up – we've got a great night sleep every single night and got our money's worth without doubt.

Room Tip: ask for a room not on the basement level.
