“An Overall Excellent Experience”
Reviewed 4 days ago NEW

We travelled to Istanbul for a weekend to celebrate our wedding anniversary and stayed at the Four Seasons Sultanahmet. If you’re in town for sightseeing, we would strongly recommend staying at this amazing property. They’ve managed to convert an old prison into a tranquil and luxurious oasis in the heart of the Old City. My wife noted that the hotel had a “fairytale setting” and it made for a very memorable experience.

Location: The excellent location of this hotel can’t be emphasized enough. All the major attractions are a short walk away: the Hagia Sophia, the Blue Mosque, the Cistern, Topkapi Palace, the Hippodrome, the Archeological Museum - it just doesn’t get any closer. The Grand Bazaar is perhaps farthest away, but even then its just a fifteen minute walk. The hotel is perfectly located on a quiet street, off the main square, so you need not worry about crowds or being disturbed by tourist traffic. It’s very hot this time of year, so a hat is recommended and dress for hot weather. The water coolers that the hotel provides at its entrance are a welcome sight after a long walk in the Old City.

Tour Guides: The Concierge recommended that we take two half-day tours instead of full-day tours and arranged a tour guide. The Concierge’s recommendation was perfect in every respect – the weather was very hot, and longer tours would have certainly been very tiring, and our tour guide, Ulku Simsek from Travelium, was an excellent guide in addition to being a very sweet person. We note that other guests have highly recommended Ulku and we cannot agree more - She did a wonderful job guiding us through the Hagia Sophia, Blue Mosque, Cistern, Topkapi Palace, Hippodrome and the Archeological Museum. We would strongly recommend utilizing a guide – in addition to providing valuable insights, state-licensed guides have priority to enter most sites, so you avoid wasting time at what could be endless lines and gain immediate entrance. One word of caution – Keep an eye out for the sometimes microscopic “do not photograph” logos at some of the historical sites, particularly at Topkapi Palace. Unlike the wonderful staff at the Four Seasons, the employees at the historical sites can be exceptionally rude if you accidentally take a snapshot in a restricted area and they were frequently yelling at visitors.

Hotel Staff: The hotel staff are very friendly and eager to assist. With one exception that I have noted below, they truly live up to the Four Seasons’ hospitality expectations, down to the finest details – from the Housekeeping staff placing a hotel bookmark in a book left in the room to the exceptional service by the Concierge. They all make you feel very welcome and at home. Selim Calik and the entire Concierge staff were exceptional – From our first telephone call to help plan our itinerary prior to our arrival until they arranged our transportation back to the airport at the end of our stay, they always provided excellent recommendations and practical advice and made all the necessary arrangements in a timely manner.

Room: The hotel overall reflects the standard of excellence that is expected from a Four Seasons property and then some. We stayed at a one-bedroom suite on the third floor and it was spectacular. Courtyard windows at most hotels may not offer the best view, but this does not apply to the Four Seasons Sultanahmet. The beautiful view of the garden in the courtyard from the third floor, set against the backdrop of the yellow building and watchtower, was truly amazing and the cool breeze you get when you open the windows only adds to the experience. Some of our windows also opened on the street and we can confirm that we did not experience any disturbance at all.

Dining: We enjoyed the two main venues at the hotel - the Seasons Restaurant, located principally in the courtyard, and the A’YA Lounge, the hotel’s rooftop terrace. The Seasons Restaurant is where breakfast, lunch and dinner are served. The food was simply amazing and the staff were very professional and attentive. We always chose to sit outside and the garden was wonderful – birds frequently stood on the chairs or the edges of the table, but did not disturb our meals in any way. It really was a beautiful setting. The A’YA Lounge provides alcoholic and non-alcoholic drinks on the rooftop terrace and the views of the Hagia Sophia and the Blue Mosque, particularly just before sunset, are nothing short of magical. The streets around the hotel also have numerous dining venues and provide for an enjoyable stroll during the evenings (the restaurant staff in the neighboring streets will always try to convince you to enter their venues, but a polite thank you usually got them to discontinue their pursuit). The Concierge also offered to recommend and arrange reservations at dining venues and even emailed us a list of preferred venues prior to our arrival.

Sightseeing Outside the Old City: The Concierge arranged a taxi for us to visit Dolmabahce Palace and recommended that we take the tram back from the nearby station. It was very enjoyable - Visitors are admitted as groups periodically with guides provided by the palace. We would recommend purchasing the ticket for both the Official Section and the Harem Section (which also includes the residence used by Ataturk). The Concierge also arranged a water taxi to visit Buyukada, the largest of the Princess Islands, on our last day at the hotel. The pier for the water taxi is a ten-minute walk from the hotel and, while significantly more expensive than the regular ferries, the water taxi provided the necessary flexibility, particularly given our flight later in the day. To be clear, its not a slow sight-seeing boat ride - you basically bounce your way in a glass enclosed speed boat for forty-five minutes each way, which I found quite enjoyable, but my wife got a little seasick. Buyukada was also very enjoyable - a horse-drawn carriage ride through the island (there is a significant line, expect at least half-an-hour to get a carriage), lunch at one of the many restaurants on the island (we would recommend those on the coast, which provide beautiful views of the sea with seagulls circling overhead) and a stroll through the local market.

Negatives: I only raise this issue for purposes of providing a complete assessment of our experience at this hotel. We informed the Four Seasons Sultanahmet at the time of reservation that we had selected their property to celebrate our wedding anniversary. I had requested the front desk to arrange the usual suite decoration with rose petals for the evening. This is usually done without request at other Four Season properties at which we have stayed and is certainly done upon request at most luxury hotels. Unfortunately, my request was ignored and never accommodated. This was very surprising and disappointing as it did not conform to the legendary service expected from any Four Seasons property. We are big Four Seasons fans and they simply do not ignore requests like this, so we're not sure what happened. Given the overall excellence of our experience at this property, I decided not to escalate the matter and settled on notifying the front desk of my disappointment. The staff were sincerely apologetic and, upon check-out, provided my wife with a flower bouquet and a complimentary gown of the type provided for use in the hotel rooms. Had it not been for this omission, which is significant by Four Seasons’ standards, our experience would have been nothing short of perfect.

Room Tip: Rooms on the higher floors are preferable.

“Wonderful service and a great location”
Reviewed 1 week ago

For our first time in Istanbul, we wanted a quality hotel in a location in the Sultanahmet area - close to major attractions.

The Four Seasons is ideally located, a few steps from the central park between the Hagia Sophia and the Blue Mosque, and situated on a quiet street away from the hustle and bustle. Also close to the Basilica Cistern and within walking distance to the Grand Bazaar, Spice Bazaar, many restaurants, and Topkapi Palace. The streetcar station is also close by, which connects you with the Galata bridge and ferries, and the other parts of Istanbul northeast of the bridge.

The hotel itself is of very high standard, even though it was a former prison. The hotel is beautifully designed with high quality furnishings. We were give a spacious room on the top floor with a balcony, with a great view of the Hagia Sophia and the Bosphorus. Large modern bathroom with all the amenities. Standards of service were high as one would expect from Four Seasons - reception, housekeeping, front doors. We took advantage of the excellent buffet breakfast every morning.

For evening dining, we always went to a street (Akbiyik) one block south of the hotel, where there was a good selection of quality restaurants serving Turkish meals. We enjoyed bantering with the restaurant staff and greatly enjoyed our meals.

We would happily return to Istanbul and the Four Seasons Sultanahmet.

Room Tip: Ask for a balcony room facing the Hagia Sophia.
